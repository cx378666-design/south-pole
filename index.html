About Antarctica

Antarctica is Earth's southernmost continent — a world of endless snow and silence. It holds 60% of the planet’s freshwater in its vast ice sheets.

Magnificent Icebergs

Floating sculptures of ice, some as big as cities, drift across Antarctic waters.

Penguins

Adorable yet resilient, penguins thrive in one of the harshest climates on Earth.

Seals & Whales

These marine giants roam freely under the shimmering ice-cold waves.

Climate & Environment

The Antarctic climate is unforgiving — strong winds, freezing temperatures, and pure white landscapes that stretch for miles.

  • Coldest temperature: -89.2°C
  • Winds over 200 km/h
  • Summer has 24-hour daylight
